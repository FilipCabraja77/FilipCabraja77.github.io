About the course

Advanced Scuba Diver

The Advanced Scuba Diver course is for certified Open Water divers who want to widen their skills through guided dives of different types, with an instructor alongside.

The course is built around six open water dives. Three of them are set: underwater navigation, a night or limited visibility dive, and a deep dive beyond 18 metres, always within recreational limits. The other three are chosen together with your instructor, so the course matches the diving you actually want to do.

After the course you are considered competent to dive without supervision, and you meet the entry requirement for the NAUI Master Scuba Diver course.

Step by step

Course content

  1. 1

    Getting started

    You need a NAUI Open Water Scuba Diver certification or the equivalent, and you must be 15 by the water phase of the course.

  2. 2

    Three required dives

    Underwater navigation, a night or limited visibility dive, and a deep dive beyond 18 metres, within recreational limits.

  3. 3

    Three chosen dives

    Three further dives selected with your instructor, to build experience across different kinds of diving.

  4. International certification

    NAUI Advanced Scuba Diver certification card

Frequently asked questions

It is for certified Open Water divers who want more experience across different types of diving, guided by an instructor.

A NAUI Open Water Scuba Diver certification or the equivalent, and a minimum age of 15 by the water phase of the course.

Six open water dives: navigation, night or limited visibility and a deep dive beyond 18 metres are required, and three more are chosen with your instructor.

Your instructor may run one screening and evaluation dive first. It does not count toward the six dives of the course.

You meet the entry requirement for the NAUI Master Scuba Diver course, the top NAUI certification in recreational diving.
